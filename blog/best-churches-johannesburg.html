Best Churches in Johannesburg by Denomination

A practical guide for “Johannesburg churches near me”

Johannesburg is a city of many communities, and churches reflect that diversity. Whether you are looking for a historic congregation, a vibrant charismatic church, or a family-focused local church, Joburg has strong options across its suburbs. This guide is organized by denomination so you can quickly find a shortlist that fits your faith tradition, then explore details via the directory.

For the full list and a map view, visit Churches in Johannesburg. You can filter by denomination and click through to individual church pages.

Anglican Churches

Anglican churches in Johannesburg are known for a rich liturgical tradition, historical roots, and community service.

  • St Mary’s Cathedral (Johannesburg) — a historic cathedral in the city center.
  • St Michael’s Anglican (Bryanston) — a strong local congregation in the north.
  • Christ Church Midrand — accessible for Midrand and nearby areas.

Catholic Churches

The Catholic community in Johannesburg is vibrant and active across many suburbs.

  • Cathedral of Christ the King — the central cathedral for the archdiocese.
  • Church of the Immaculate Conception (Rosebank) — a longstanding parish in Rosebank.

Methodist Churches

Methodist churches in Johannesburg are known for warm community and practical teaching.

  • Bryanston Methodist Church — a strong congregation in the north.
  • Rosebank Methodist Church — close to the Rosebank and Parktown areas.
  • Randburg Methodist Church — convenient for central and northern suburbs.
  • Alberton Methodist Church — for congregants in the south-east.

Baptist Churches

Baptist churches emphasize Bible teaching, discipleship, and local church leadership.

  • Heritage Baptist Church — a well-known congregation with strong teaching.
  • Honeyridge Baptist Church — serving the Randpark Ridge community.
  • Fourways Baptist Church — a north-side church for families.
  • Northcliff Baptist Church — known for local outreach and teaching.
  • Edenvale Baptist Church — serving Edenvale and surrounding suburbs.
  • Roodepoort Baptist Church — a practical option in the west.

Presbyterian Churches

Presbyterian churches often combine historic worship with strong community life.

  • St Columba’s Presbyterian (Parkview) — a landmark in the Parkview area.

Lutheran Churches

Lutheran communities are smaller but deeply rooted in theological tradition.

  • Benoni Lutheran Church — convenient for the east and surrounding areas.

Seventh-day Adventist

Adventist churches have a distinct focus on Sabbath worship and holistic community life.

  • Sandton Seventh-day Adventist Church — a central SDA option for northern suburbs.

Pentecostal and Charismatic Churches

Johannesburg has a large Pentecostal and charismatic community, ranging from megachurches to local congregations. These churches often feature contemporary worship, dynamic teaching, and strong community outreach.

  • Rhema Bible Church — one of the largest and most well-known churches in SA.
  • Rivers Church Sandton — modern worship and a large community.
  • Grace Bible Church (Johannesburg) — a widely recognized charismatic congregation.
  • Christian Revival Church (CRC) — a national movement with a strong Johannesburg presence.
  • Cornerstone Church Johannesburg — a city-focused community with Bible-centered teaching.

Non-Denominational and Community Churches

Non-denominational churches are often built around strong community and accessible teaching.

  • Rosebank Union Church — a longstanding community church with global mission focus.

Dutch Reformed (NG Kerk)

The Dutch Reformed tradition remains strong in Johannesburg, particularly across suburban communities.

  • NG Kerk Randburg — a historic congregation serving Randburg.
  • NG Kerk Sandton — located in the heart of Sandton.

How to Choose the Right Church in Johannesburg

If you are new to Johannesburg or simply exploring, use this process to choose a good fit:

  • Pick 3 churches within 20–30 minutes of your home or work.
  • Visit each church twice to see if it’s consistent and community-focused.
  • Speak to a leader or newcomers’ team about groups and serving opportunities.

Most people find a church by combining location, worship style, and the sense of community they experience in their first few visits. The directory makes that process easier.
